# The Principle of Atomic Notes

One note, one idea. This is the atomic note principle.

Keeping each note small and focused means the same note can be reused across different contexts. A large note becomes "a note about that topic," but an atomic note becomes the idea itself.

A good size guideline: one title, one claim in the body. The ideal density is what lets you grasp the content instantly when you revisit it.

Connections between notes should be built not by topic similarity but by **flow of thought** — "this idea depends on that idea," or "this idea challenges that one." That kind of linking grows a network of knowledge rather than a pile of summaries.
