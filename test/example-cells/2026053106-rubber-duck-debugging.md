# Rubber Duck Debugging

Explaining a problem out loud — even to an inanimate object — often reveals the solution.

The act of articulating forces you to slow down and examine each assumption.
This is why [[2026053103]] is so effective: writing forces the same kind of articulation.

## Why it works

- Language requires sequential, explicit reasoning.
- Gaps in understanding become audible (or visible on the page).
- The "audience" creates a mild social pressure that sharpens focus.

## In practice

Keep a rubber duck (physical or metaphorical) near your workspace.
Before asking for help, explain the problem step by step to the duck first.
