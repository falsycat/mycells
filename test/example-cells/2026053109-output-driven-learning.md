# Output-Driven Learning

Learning by producing something — an essay, a talk, a project — is more effective
than passive consumption alone.

The act of producing forces retrieval and reveals gaps that reading does not.

## Why output matters

[[2026053103]] argues that writing clarifies thinking.
Output-driven learning extends this: the goal of learning is not to know, but to do.

## Practical forms

- Write a short explanation of what you just read.
- Teach the concept to someone else.
- Build a small prototype that uses the idea.
- Connect it to existing notes using [[2026053102]].
